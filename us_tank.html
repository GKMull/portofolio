1935 M1 Combat car – 113 built. Armament 2 30. Cal machine gun

while deceptively name it is a tankette. While similar to the M2 it was built for the cavalry while the m2 was made for the infantry. after Spanish civil war it was obsolete and drop from service.

M2 Light tank, - 989 built 1935 – 42. Armament 37mm cannon and 5 .30 Cal mg or 1 .50cal and. 30 cal Mgs.

From twin turreted tankette to A4 modification with a 37mm cannon this tank would be modified and adapted from the lessons learn from the Spanish civil war.

1939 M2 Medium – 112 1939 armament 37mm cannon and 7 .30 Cals mg. while a moving machine gun bunkers after the invasion of Poland production would cease to make way some able to compete with the Germans tanks.

1941 M3/M5 Stuart light – 22,744 1941-44 armament 37mm cannon, 2- 3 .30 Cals mgs

The Stuart tanks were improved versions of the m2 light the m3 having thicker armor and m5 having improved redesigned front hull armor.

M3 lee medium 6,258 1941-42 armament 75mm, 37mm, 2-4 Cal .30 mgs.

This was a stop gap tank while the m4 was still in development combining any element might deemed useful into one thing. This Frankenstein monster tank had a much longer production because changing the line to m4 was not considered efficient enough.

1942 M4 Sherman medium 49,324 1942-45, armament 75-76 mm n or 105mm howitzer 2 .30 Cals 1 .50 Cal.

The Sherman was the universal tank, the jack of all trades master of none. It fought on all fronts. One of the most produced tanks. With many different iterations. It stories of it development exemplifies how America became a superpower in a few years.

1943 M22 locust light 1943-44 830 built, armament 37 mm cannon, .30 Cal mg.

Designed as an air deployable tank, it light armor made it obsolete before it rolled off the production line. While not used by US force it was given to the British and used once in Operation varsity.

1944 M24 Chaffee Light 1944-45 4730 built, armament 75mm cannon, .50 Cal, .30 cal mg

The Chaffee while put into action late in WWII was still a mass upgrade compared to it’s Stuart counter parts, But many units didn’t this tank till after the war. It best know for being in the Korean war having to facing off enemy tanks with better armor and fire power.

M26 Pershing Heavy/Medium 2,202 1944-45 built, armament 90mm Cannon, 2 .30cal mg .50 cal mg

The Pershing was the only American heavy tank to see combat as other programs cancelled or never made it to a war, but after WWII the Pershing was redesignated as a medium tank due to changing standers of tanks, the reason that us never field any other heavy tanks is due to lack of proper infrastructure only a handful of train cars and cranes could support the weight. So, when this tanks rolled on to the battlefield it wasn’t just a sign tank development but the industrial capability of the US.

1949 M46 Patton Medium 1,168 built 1949-51, armament 90mm Cannon 2 .30cal mg .50 cal mg.

Bulit during the Korean war The M46 is a modernized M26 with most notably a new 800hp engine. It was also the first American Main Battle tank (MBT), A new class of tank with more powerful engines allow for bigger and heavier tanks without sacrificing mobility.

1951 M41 Walker Bulldog light 5,467 built 1951-54, armament 76mm cannon, 1 .50 and 1 .30 cal mg

With new munitions types the m41 was able to US light tanks to a modern fire power standard, and Replacing any other light tanks by 1953

1951 M47 Patton 8,576 built 1951-54, armament 90mm Cannon, .30cal mg, 2 .50 Cal mg.

While made in large quantity to replace the problematic m46, it would never see service in us use, beginning replaced by m48 and m103 tanks and retired 1959.

1952 M48 Patton 11,703 built 1952-61, armament 90 - 105mm Cannon, .30cal mg, .50 Cal mg.

M48 Patton would be the first us tank to use the current crew lay out Driver in the center hull command seated behind the gunner on the right side and loader on the left with ammo behind him. The m48 was chosen over the m47 due to the turret being wider allowing for more future modification like 105mm L7 cannon that would be on the m60.

1953 M103 Heavy Tank 300 built 1953-54 armament 120mm cannon 2 .30, cal, 1 50 cal mg.

Bulit in response to the USSR heavy tanks, it would quickly fall out of favor with the army, due to being too fragile and troublesome. The remaining m103 would stay with marine corps until hope ran out for a m60 replacements.

1959 M60 Medium Tank 15,000 built 1959-83 armament 105 mm L7 cannon, .50,.30 cal mg

When first produced it had a m48 turret, few years later to the wedge turret giving much better protection it was also the first tank to feature Era (Explosive Reactive armor)

1966 M551 Sheridan light 1,662 built 1966-70 armament 152mm gun launcher, .50,.30 cal mg.

The Sheridan had aluminum for the hull to be used for air droppable. It also had a gun launcher system for the main armament, allowing it to lob large he rounds for direct fire support for infantry, it replaced the m41. its biggest problem was its gun with heavy recoil and short lifespan, the missile developed for it was produced but rarely used. It was considered to been better if the gun was made to be a howitzer.

1979 M1 Abrams MBT 10,400 built 1979-92. Armament 105-120, .50,.30 cal mg

Currently the only tank in US service features a 1500hp turbine jet engine with a .6 mpg. First built with a 105 cannon to be in step with the m60 but soon upgraded to a 120mm cannon being the a1 variant. The M1 designation comes from being the first tank equipped with composite armor in the US. The latest version is the is the M1A2 sep v3 with a v4 in the works.
