Home page, What is a tank

[What is a tank, as the war in Ukraine rages on people have on the news and media have falsely claimed that many different combat vehicles are tanks. But what is the cause of all this confusion, one reason is because it looks like a tank combine with lack knowledge. Second, people like to overvalue things by calling it a tank without caring about correct. Or giving special terms to create interest from the public, to generate revenue.

So how do we know what a tank is. A quick google give us a heavy armored fighting vehicle carrying guns and moving on a continuous articulated metal track. It does. It does sound like the tank in your head, but it is too generic as (image to left) is a m3 tank while the (image to the right) is a m7 Howitzer Motor Carriage both fulfill the definition, but one is not a tank even though they share design DNA. So, what defines a tank. actually, it’s doctrine that defines what a tank is.

While multiple countries have tanks, they also have different definitions and roles though out history. cruiser tanks in the UK would be light or medium tanks and vice versa for infantry tanks and assault tanks. It might seem like these things might seem miner, but definition and doctrine will create the expectation for what will it be and what, creating the variation between tanks though out history. But still there are some ways to define a tank. 1. Main Arment must a large caliber canon. Not howitzer (unless modified) or auto canon (fast fire guns would be a tankette)

2. Must be a full track vehicle.

3. Must have some level armor. Even light tanks should be able to withstand machine gun fire.

4. Must have a turret. Unless it is to fulfill all role to it counterpart (strv-103)

5. Was not made to carry infantry in to battle. While not perfect, these rules should allow you to quickly filter out any non-tanks.

[The anatomy of a tank]

[The two main areas of the tank are the turret and the hull, which are subdivided to other major components. in the hull including engine and gas, suspension, tracks, and additional ammo storage while turret has the ready rack, mantle, cannon, and sights.

In the back of the hull lies the engine if not the engine and transmission it would be additional ammo storage, in the past fuel tanks were in back but modern tanks it used as additional layer of armor around the tank while in the turret would be the ready rack ammo and the radio.

in the center of the tank is the turret ring the gears where turret rotates on. Inside of the ring is the turret basket, it holds the gunner commander and loader keeping aligned with the turret. Different tanks have different crew layouts but the 3 crew in the turret is the norm for NATO tanks while 2 crew for Russian and other autoloader tanks. In old tank mostly German and American, there may be a powertrain connecting the engine in back to the transition in front.

On the bottom of the tank is the track, wheels, and suspension, for the wheel on the ground are the road wheels they hold the weight, the gear looking wheel is the drive wheel it gives motion to the tank, on the opposite side of it is the idler wheel giving the track a constant shape ground presser. And the return wheels keep the track up and out of the way. Suspension is a dependent (multiple wheels per suspension) or independent (single wheel suspension)

In front before ammo being in front was put anywhere while in front hull gunner or assistant driver / hull gunner next to the driver instead of the ammo, the lower and upper plates of armor is called upper and lower glacis respectively. The centered cannon (the best cannon layout) next to it is often a co-axial machine gun. The moving part of the turret face around the cannon is the mantlet while the side of turret of the front are the cheeks. The sighting of a tank gunner’s next to the cannon in the mantlet or on top of the turret. The commanders is on top and can freely rotate. ]
